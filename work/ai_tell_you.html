(A) I Tell You, You Tell Me

ZKM, Flatware Hardware. Wetware software Exhibition, 2024

AI Tell You / You Tell Me is an interactive media installation that questions the authority of interpretation in art. As museums and cultural institutions increasingly adopt algorithmic tools to describe and organize their collections, this work confronts a central question: Who owns the truth when artificial intelligence becomes the voice of meaning? :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Concept

A selection of original artworks from the ZKM collection hang on the walls, each accompanied by a small Raspberry Pi‑powered screen. Instead of fixed museum labels, these screens display AI‑generated interpretations—uniquely tailored for each visitor. Visitors receive an RFID tag and go to an interaction station where they answer philosophical, aesthetic, and emotional questions. Based on these inputs, the system selects one of 16 conceptual themes such as nostalgia, hyper‑capitalism, utopia, or paranoia. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Technical Write‑Up

The AI system integrates custom‑trained vision models that extract semantic features from artworks and produce text aligned with the selected theme. A theme‑selection engine dynamically maps visitor input to narrative filters that generate interpretations that are both technically sound and philosophically provocative. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Context

AI Tell You / You Tell Me explores the consequences of turning interpretive power over to machines—especially in institutions trusted as custodians of cultural knowledge. By making viewers co‑authors of meaning, it invites reflection on how truth is shaped in the age of AI. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
